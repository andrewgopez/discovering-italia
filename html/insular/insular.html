Insular Italy (Italia Insulare)

Insular Italy (Italia Insulare) consists of the country’s two largest islands: Sicily and Sardinia. Surrounded by the Mediterranean Sea, these islands have developed unique identities shaped by centuries of contact with different civilizations, including the Greeks, Phoenicians, Romans, Arabs, and Spanish. Known for their stunning landscapes, archaeological sites, traditional foods, and rich cultural heritage, the islands represent some of Italy’s most diverse regions.
